# Tomato Early Blight

Symptoms:
- Brown spots on older tomato leaves.
- Spots may have rings like a target.
- Leaves turn yellow around the spots and may dry.
- Disease is often worse after rain, overhead irrigation, or heavy dew.

Immediate action:
- Remove badly affected lower leaves and destroy them away from the field.
- Avoid overhead watering.
- Improve spacing and pruning so leaves dry faster.
- Mulch to reduce soil splash onto leaves.

Treatment:
- If symptoms keep spreading, ask a trusted agrovet for a registered tomato fungicide such as copper or mancozeb.
- Follow the product label, pre-harvest interval, dosage, and protective equipment instructions.

Prevention:
- Rotate tomatoes with non-solanaceous crops.
- Stake tomatoes and avoid overcrowding.
- Do not work in the crop when leaves are wet.

Safety:
- Do not mix chemicals without label guidance.
- If the attack is severe, contact an extension officer or verified crop expert.
