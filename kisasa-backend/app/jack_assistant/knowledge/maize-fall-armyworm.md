# Maize Fall Armyworm

Symptoms:
- Ragged holes on maize leaves.
- Fresh feeding damage in the whorl.
- Sawdust-like frass inside the whorl.
- Caterpillars may hide deep inside young plants.

Immediate action:
- Scout early in the morning or late afternoon.
- Check at least 20 plants in different parts of the field.
- Hand-pick and destroy caterpillars when infestation is still low.

Treatment:
- If many plants are affected, ask a trusted agrovet for a registered fall armyworm product.
- Apply into the whorl where the pest is feeding.
- Follow label dosage, safety, and harvest interval instructions.

Prevention:
- Plant early where possible.
- Keep checking young maize weekly.
- Encourage natural enemies by avoiding unnecessary broad-spectrum spraying.
